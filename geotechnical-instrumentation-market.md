<p><p>The geotechnical instrumentation market is divided into wired and wireless segments. Wired instrumentation relies on physical connections for data transmission, offering reliable and accurate measurements, often used in permanent installations. In contrast, wireless instrumentation utilizes radio frequency or other technologies, providing flexibility and ease of installation, making it suitable for temporary setups or remote locations. Each type has its advantages; wired solutions are preferred for stability and durability, while wireless options excel in mobility and reduced installation complexity.</p></p>
