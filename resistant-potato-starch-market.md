<p><p>Resistant potato starch (RPS) is categorized into two main types: RS2 and RS3. RS2 is naturally occurring in raw potatoes, characterized by its granular form that resists digestion, promoting digestive health. RS3, on the other hand, is the retrograded form that forms when cooked and then cooled, enhancing its resistant properties. Both types serve as prebiotics, supporting gut health and improving blood sugar management. Their growing demand in food products and supplements reflects a shift towards healthier dietary options.</p></p>
