<p><p>The hopper barge market is divided into two main types: propelled and non-propelled. Propelled hopper barges are equipped with their own engines, allowing for greater maneuverability and autonomy in navigation. Non-propelled hopper barges, on the other hand, rely on tugboats for movement, making them more dependent on external towing services. Each type serves distinct operational needs in transporting bulk materials, with propelled barges often being preferred for routes requiring flexibility, while non-propelled barges are typically favored for cost efficiency in larger fleets.</p></p>
