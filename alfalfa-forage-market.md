<p><p>Alfalfa forage is primarily categorized into dried and wet pulp markets based on moisture content and processing methods. Dried pulp consists of sun-dried or dehydrated alfalfa, providing a concentrated, shelf-stable feed for livestock, rich in nutrients. Conversely, wet pulp retains higher moisture, often used immediately after processing for its fresh nutritional content, enhancing palatability for animals. These market types cater to different feeding strategies, with dried pulp favored for long-term storage and wet pulp preferred for immediate consumption.</p></p>
