<p><ul><li>Isotropic Graphite</li><li>Extruded Graphite</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The flexible graphite market comprises mainly two types: isotropic graphite and extruded graphite. Isotropic graphite is characterized by uniform properties in all directions, which enhances its thermal and electrical conductivity, making it suitable for high-performance applications. In contrast, extruded graphite is produced through a molding process, offering good mechanical strength and excellent thermal properties. Both types are widely used in gaskets, seals, and heat exchangers, catering to industries such as automotive, aerospace, and electronics.</p></p>
