<p><p>The arthritic therapeutic market is divided into two main categories: biologics and non-biologics. Biologics are derived from living organisms and target specific components of the immune system to reduce inflammation and joint damage in conditions like rheumatoid arthritis. Examples include monoclonal antibodies and fusion proteins. Non-biologics, on the other hand, encompass traditional disease-modifying antirheumatic drugs (DMARDs), nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs), and analgesics, which focus on alleviating symptoms and slowing disease progression without targeting specific biological pathways.</p></p>
