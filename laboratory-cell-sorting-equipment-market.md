<p><ul><li>Fluorescent Activated Cell Sorting</li><li>Magnetic-activated Cell Sorting</li><li>MEMS - Microfluidics</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Laboratory cell sorting equipment encompasses various technologies that facilitate the isolation of specific cell types for analysis and research. Fluorescent Activated Cell Sorting (FACS) uses fluorescent markers to identify and separate cells based on their properties. Magnetic-Activated Cell Sorting (MACS) relies on magnetic beads to isolate target cells from a mixture. MEMS-based microfluidics offer miniaturized systems that manipulate fluids and cells at the microscale, allowing for precise sorting and analysis in a compact format, enhancing efficiency and accuracy in cell studies.</p></p>
