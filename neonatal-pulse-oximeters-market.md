<p><p>The neonatal pulse oximeters market consists of two primary types: portable pulse oximeters and bedside/tabletop pulse oximeters. Portable pulse oximeters are compact, lightweight devices designed for on-the-go monitoring, allowing healthcare providers to assess oxygen saturation in newborns outside hospital settings. In contrast, bedside or tabletop pulse oximeters are larger, stationary units integrated into neonatal intensive care units, providing continuous monitoring and advanced features for critically ill infants. Both types play crucial roles in ensuring timely interventions for neonatal patients.</p></p>
