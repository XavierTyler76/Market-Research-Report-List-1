<p><p>The microfluidic equipment market can be categorized into three main types: glass, polymer, and silicon. Glass microfluidics offers excellent chemical resistance and optical clarity, making it ideal for high-precision applications, particularly in research and diagnostics. Polymer microfluidics is cost-effective and easier to manufacture, suitable for disposable devices and high-throughput applications. Silicon microfluidics provides exceptional integration with electronic components, enabling advanced sensing and control in the development of lab-on-a-chip technologies. Each material type serves distinct applications based on their unique properties.</p></p>
